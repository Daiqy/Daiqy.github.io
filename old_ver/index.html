About Me

I am a research assistant at the Center on Frontiers of Computing Studies, Peking University, and also a research intern at the Embodied AI center of Beijing Academy of Artificial Intelligence, under the supervision of Prof. He Wang. Before that, I received my M.S. from Peking University in 2022, supervised by Prof. He Wang and Prof. Qi Jing, and B.E. from Wuhan University in 2019, supervised by Prof. Qin Zou.

I am enthusiastic about investigating the confluence of 3D vision, computer graphics, and artificial intelligence, with a particular focus on neural implicit representations that capture the visual, geometric, and physical attributes of our surroundings. My goal is to develop highly realistic virtual environments that enable immersive experiences in VR/AR and facilitate the training of embodied agents capable of generalizing to real-world scenarios.

I am in my gap year, and seeking a Ph.D. position in the field of neural scene representation and neural rendering, ideally starting in either 2023 or 2024.
